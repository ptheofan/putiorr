Combined put.io and local transfer state from the durable SQLite queue.

putiorr makes put.io look like a local Transmission download client. Your *arr apps send releases to putiorr, put.io downloads them in the cloud, then putiorr copies the finished files into a folder your *arr app imports from — no local seeding, no torrent client to babysit.

1

Your app grabs a release

Sonarr, Radarr or Lidarr sends the magnet or .torrent to putiorr over the Transmission RPC protocol — putiorr looks like an ordinary download client. Each app uses its own RR profile and category.

2

putiorr queues it on put.io

The bridge forwards the transfer to your connected put.io account using the put.io API, into the destination folder set on the profile.

3

put.io downloads in the cloud

put.io fetches and seeds the torrent remotely. The dashboard shows live put.io progress, including the Completing phase while it finalises.

4

Files land where the app looks

Once ready, putiorr copies the files into the shared download folder. Your app sees the completed download at that exact path and imports it. A Download profile controls slow-speed reset during the copy.
